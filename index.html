Plasma Physics

Plasma Physics is the study of plasma, the fourth state of matter where gases are ionized with free electrons and ions. It explores how plasma behaves under the influence of electric and magnetic fields and is crucial in fields ranging from astrophysics to industrial applications like nuclear fusion and plasma cutting.

Quantum Theory

Quantum Theory is a fundamental framework in physics that describes the behavior of matter and energy at the smallest scales, such as atoms and subatomic particles. It introduces concepts like quantization of energy, wave-particle duality, and the uncertainty principle, fundamentally challenging classical notions of physics and providing a deeper understanding of the nature and behavior of the physical world.

Deep Learning

Deep Learning is a subset of machine learning based on artificial neural networks with representation learning. It involves networks with many layers (hence "deep") that process data in complex ways, learning features and tasks directly from vast amounts of data. Deep learning is particularly powerful for tasks like image recognition, natural language processing, and playing complex games, where it can identify patterns and make decisions.

Numerical Analysis

Numerical Analysis is the field of mathematics and computer science that creates, analyzes, and implements algorithms for solving mathematically formulated problems numerically. This includes approximations for differential equations, integrals, and optimization problems where analytical solutions are difficult or impossible to obtain. It's crucial in engineering, physics, finance, and other sciences where precise calculations are needed.

X-Ray Diffraction Analysis

X-Ray Diffraction Analysis is a technique used to study the atomic or molecular structure of a material. By directing X-rays at a sample and observing the patterns of rays scattered by the material's atoms, scientists can infer the structure and arrangement of those atoms. This method is widely used in materials science, chemistry, and biology to identify crystalline structures and analyze various substances at the atomic level.

Thin Film Deposition

This process is essential in the manufacture of semiconductors, solar panels, and various optical and electrical devices. Techniques like chemical vapor deposition (CVD), physical vapor deposition (PVD), and atomic layer deposition (ALD) are commonly used, each differing in how the material is deposited to form the film and tailored for specific material properties and application requirements.

Sputtering

Sputtering is a widely used technique for thin film deposition. It involves ejecting material from a "target" material by bombarding it with energetic particles, usually ions in a plasma. The ejected material then travels through a vacuum and deposits onto a substrate, forming a thin film. Sputtering is used for coating materials in applications ranging from microelectronics and solar cells to the protective coatings on hard drives and optical components. This method is favored for its ability to uniformly coat large and complex-shaped surfaces.
